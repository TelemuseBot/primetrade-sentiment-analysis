# Trader Performance vs. Bitcoin Market Sentiment

Data science assignment for **Primetrade.ai** — exploring the relationship between
Bitcoin market sentiment (Fear/Greed Index) and trader performance on Hyperliquid.

## 📌 Objective

Merge trader-level execution data with daily Bitcoin sentiment classification to:
- Quantify how PnL, win rate, and trade behaviour shift across sentiment regimes
- Identify long/short bias patterns tied to Fear vs Greed
- Surface symbol-level and trader-level patterns
- Translate findings into actionable trading-strategy implications

## 🔑 Key Finding

Traders in this dataset were **consistently more profitable during Fear than
Greed** — total closed PnL was ~$3.15M during Fear regimes vs ~$1.49M during
Greed regimes, and win rate/PnL-per-trade decline in a near-monotonic pattern
from Extreme Fear toward Extreme Greed. Full breakdown, charts, and strategy
implications are in the [PDF report](report/Trader_Sentiment_Analysis_Report.pdf).

## 🧠 Methodology (short version)

- Trade timestamps converted to calendar dates and joined to the daily
  sentiment classification (Extreme Fear → Extreme Greed).
- Only position-**closing** trades (`Direction` containing "Close") carry a
  realized `Closed PnL`; all win-rate/profitability metrics use this subset.
  Trade-size and buy/sell-mix metrics use the full trade set (opens + closes).
- Metrics computed per sentiment bucket: total/avg PnL, win rate, avg trade
  size, buy/sell mix, symbol-level PnL, and trader-level leaderboard.
- Correlation between the raw sentiment score (0–100) and aggregate daily
  closed PnL was also computed.

## ⚠️ Caveats

Results are observational, not causal — sentiment may proxy for volatility
or liquidity conditions that independently affect PnL. See the "Caveats"
section in the PDF report for the full list.
